# FailOften Meeting Agenda - ZAO Festivals Brand + New Media Commons

Draft agenda. Date TBD. Participants: Zaal, FailOften. Optionally Adam for the commercial block (see note).

## Why this meeting

ZAOstock is live and public (Luma up at ticket.zaostock.com, pitch page shipped, first crowdfunding round open). Before the festival series scales past ZAOstock, the brand and the money structure need to be locked at the ZAO Festivals level - not just the ZAOstock level. FailOften owns the New Media Commons + ENTERACT relationship, so this is the conversation to settle the structural questions.

Goal of the meeting: walk out with the ZAO Festivals brand model and the fiscal/commercial structure clear enough that ZAOville, ZAOstock, and 2027 chapters all run on the same rails.

## Pre-read - what is already locked

So we do not re-litigate settled things:

- **Two-path money flow** is locked (FailOften 2026-05-08). Public path: donor -> Fractured Atlas (6-8% admin) -> New Media Commons -> ENTERACT (2% treasury) -> production. Commercial path: client/sponsor -> ENTERACT (2% treasury) -> production, 50% upfront / 50% before delivery.
- **ZAO is NOT itself fiscally sponsored.** NMC is the fiscally sponsored project of Fractured Atlas. ENTERACT is separate production support. Never say "ZAO is a nonprofit" or "ZAO has fiscal sponsorship."
- **ENTERACT gate process:** Define -> Greenlight -> Assign -> Execute -> Deliver. No funding = no project.
- **Team finders + management fee:** 5% finders + 10% management = 15% combined, separate from infrastructure, a project budget line item.
- **Chapter count:** ZAO-PALOOZA (NYC, Apr 2024), ZAO-CHELLA (Miami, Dec 2024), ZAOville (DMV, Jul 2026), ZAOstock (Maine, Oct 2026). ZAOstock is chapter 4. Only ZAO-PALOOZA broke even.
- **Brand umbrella:** local sponsorships go out under "The ZAO."

This agenda is about what is NOT settled.

## Block 1 - ZAO Festivals as a brand (15 min)

The series has 4 chapters but no settled answer for how the brand actually works as a unit.

Discuss:
- Who owns the ZAO Festivals brand? Is there a parent entity, or is it a label that The ZAO applies to events?
- Is each chapter standalone, or do they share infrastructure (livestream rig, sponsor framework, run-of-show playbook, partner templates)?
- The /event-organizers page publicly invites people to "run your own ZAO" under the ZAO Festivals umbrella. What is the actual model when an outside organizer wants in - licensing, franchise, revenue split, brand-use agreement? FailOften's input needed on the fiscal/legal side of letting an outsider operate under the brand.
- ZAOville is co-hosted with DCoop. What is the precedent that sets for co-hosted chapters - revenue split, brand control, who carries liability?

Decide: a one-paragraph definition of what "ZAO Festivals" is as a brand and entity, that we can put in the pitch and the organizer page.

---

## Block 2 - How ZAO Festivals can work better (15 min)

The series has run on improvisation. What needs to be systematized so chapter 5+ is not a cold start each time.

Discuss:
- What is the repeatable ZAOstock playbook (run-of-show, sponsor framework, fiscal setup, livestream, partner template) and who owns keeping it current?
- The DCoop July test run - we are applying ZAOstock musician onboarding to DCoop's event as a repeatability test. What else should the test cover so we learn the right things before October?
- Where does ENTERACT's gate process plug into a chapter's lifecycle - at what point does a new chapter hit "Greenlight"?
- Cadence: Zaal moved to 2-3x/week syncs with Adam. What is the right FailOften cadence - this meeting should set it.

Decide: the minimum shared playbook + who owns it, and the FailOften check-in cadence.

---

## Block 3 - New Media Commons - operational questions (15 min)

The two-path flow is locked. The operational details are not.

Open questions for FailOften:
- What exactly is eligible to be administered through NMC for a ZAO Festivals chapter - artist pay, venue, production, all of it, or only certain categories?
- Does the NMC relationship cover ALL ZAO Festivals chapters, or is it per-chapter / per-initiative? If ZAOville wants the public path, is that automatic or a new setup?
- The Pro Ticket crowdfunding round ($50 x 20 = $1,000) is currently routed direct (thezao.eth + PayPal to zaalpanthaki). Should crowdfunding money go through NMC for the tax-deductible benefit, or is direct fine for small community crowdfunding? What is the threshold where it should switch to the public path?
- Reporting - what does NMC/FA need from us, and when? Anything we should be tracking now so a future grant application is clean?
- Timeline - if a foundation or grant-maker wants to fund ZAOstock via the public path, how long does the FA/NMC pipeline take? We need to know the lead time before we pitch grant-makers.

Decide: a clear "this goes through NMC / this stays direct" rule, and the reporting cadence FailOften needs.

---

## Block 4 - Commercial model + backing (15 min)

Adam offered to build a ZAOstock commercial model as a volunteer and may be able to line up backing/financing (see ZAO OS research doc 599). This block overlaps with the tracked 3-way Zaal + Adam + FailOften conversation - consider pulling Adam in for this block, or have Zaal brief FailOften and run it async.

Discuss:
- If Adam lines up "backing" or "financing" for the festival - how does outside investment interact with the fiscal sponsorship structure? Can a fiscally-sponsored project take investment, or does that money have to be structured as commercial sponsorship / a separate vehicle?
- The 3 sponsor tiers (day-of physical, online broadcast, year-round) - does each tier work cleanly on both money paths, or does tier choice constrain the path?
- Who decides which path a sponsor uses - the sponsor, or us? The locked rule says "the sponsor picks," but for backing/financing that may not hold.
- ZAOstock as a loss leader: if year 1 runs at a loss by design, what is the entity that absorbs that, and what is the path to year 2 sustainability?

Decide: a clear answer on whether outside backing is even compatible with the current structure, before Adam goes and finds it.

---

## Block 5 - AutoCo / incorporation (10 min)

Zaal plans to use AutoCo to incorporate the brand. This needs to be reconciled with the NMC fiscal sponsorship before it happens.

Discuss:
- If The ZAO / ZAO Festivals incorporates as its own entity via AutoCo, does it still need or want the NMC fiscal sponsorship? Or does the incorporated entity run the commercial path while NMC stays the public path?
- Does incorporating change anything about how ENTERACT relates to ZAO Festivals?
- Sequencing - does incorporation happen before or after ZAOstock? What is the trigger?

Decide: whether incorporation and fiscal sponsorship coexist or one replaces the other, and the sequencing.
